# Regulatory and Homologation Baseline

## Scope

This file records regulation-level facts and the captured Ford GT 40 FIA research-copy baseline for the 1968 period. It is not a substitute for an ASN-certified event copy, chassis records, team preparation sheets, or component manuals.

Primary local sources:

- S009, FIA Appendix J 1968 English PDF.
- S014, FIA Historic Database cars-list guidance for HTP and homologation-form document control.
- S015, FIA Ford GT 40 Recognition/Homologation Form No. 224 Group 4 and linked PDF research copy.
- S027, Ford Heritage Vault press-release PDF for Ford-authored GT40 racing-history context.

## Verified 1968 FIA Appendix J baseline

- Group 4 sat under recognized-production sportscars for the 1968 period.
- Group 4 sportscar recognition depended on a 50-car threshold.
- Sportscars required at least two seats.
- Cylinder-capacity classes included over 3,000 cc to 5,000 cc, which is the relevant regulation class for a 302 cu in / about 4.9 L Ford small-block configuration.
- Recognition forms were official control documents and could be required at scrutineering or at the start of an event.

S027 independently gives Ford press-release context that the FIA capped engine displacement at 5 liters before the Gulf-sponsored Mark I GT40 Le Mans wins in 1968 and 1969. Treat S009/S015 as the controlling technical/regulatory sources; S027 is historical support, not a regulation text or homologation form.

## Verified Ford GT 40 homologation baseline

Source S015 captures the official FIA Historic Database record for Ford GT 40, Recognition/Homologation No. 224, Group 4. The linked form is a research copy only; S014 still controls event use and requires an ASN-certified copy for HTP/event documentation.

Extracted S015 baseline:

- Ford GT 40, Group 4, 4736 cc, RWD, 8 cylinders, United Kingdom, homologated February 2, 1966.
- Ford Advanced Vehicles Ltd. manufacturer context and 50 identical cars reached by December 31, 1965.
- 95 in / 2413 mm wheelbase.
- 55 in / 1397 mm front track and rear track.
- 905 kg / 1993 lb recognized weight under the form's stated definition.
- Partly unitized steel chassis/body structure, steel unitary material, fiberglass and steel coachwork.
- Base 289 cu in / 4736 cc V8 with cast-iron block and heads.
- Base ZF 5-speed gearbox recognition entry.
- 1968 variant/evolution entries for Weslake aluminium cylinder heads, 4942.16 cc / 302 cu in engine, Hewland LG600 5-speed gearbox, and Ford heavy-duty limited-slip differential.

## System implications

### Chassis and body

S015 now provides the GT40 No. 224 research-copy baseline for body material, wheelbase, track, and recognized weight. This does not identify the user's car or prove that its tub, clips, doors, or repairs match the form. For restoration, record chassis identity, tub details, panel construction, and as-found weight data before altering structure or bodywork.

### Engine and induction

Engine identity is a regulated configuration item. For this project, the Gurney-Weslake 302 label is not enough. Capture block casting/date, bore, stroke, crankshaft-bearing arrangement, head markings, valve/chamber data, cam card, induction, ignition, and build-sheet evidence.

Appendix J also treats ignition, induction, exhaust, carburettor, filters, and manifolds as freer than the hard engine identity points for Group 4 recognition. This is a legality/configuration distinction, not a tuning instruction. The installed hardware and engine build still control safe settings.

S015 verifies that the 1968 GT40 recognition package included a Weslake aluminium cylinder-head variant and a 4942.16 cc / 302 cu in engine evolution. It does not prove the installed block, crankshaft, heads, cam, induction, or ignition match those entries.

### Driveline

The regulation distinguishes controlled gearbox identity from freer ratio selection. Do not infer a ZF specification, oil, preload, final drive, or gear stack from period GT40 context until the actual transaxle is identified.

S015 verifies a base ZF 5-speed recognition entry and a 1968 evolution for a Hewland LG600 5-speed gearbox plus Ford heavy-duty limited-slip differential. It does not prove which unit is installed in the car.

### Suspension and steering

Suspension principle and component function are recognition-sensitive. Preserve shim packs, pickup-point evidence, steering-arm details, upright markings, damper/spring identification, and any historic setup notes before changing geometry.

### Brakes, wheels, and tires

The regulation baseline expects a four-wheel brake system with retained braking after a partial transmission-system leakage or failure. This supports careful hydraulic-system inspection, but it does not provide GT40-specific master-cylinder, bias, pad, disc, or fluid settings.

### Period safety benchmark

Appendix J includes additional safety provisions for two-seater racing cars: fire extinguisher, scatter shield where clutch/flywheel failure can endanger the driver, roll bar, safety belt, firewall/floor protection, tank isolation, enclosed batteries, circuit breaker, and fuel-tank filling/venting rules. These are retained as period safety benchmarks. They do not replace the exact GT40 homologation form or current event rules.
