# Engine and Induction

## Scope

Ford Windsor-derived 302 short block, Gurney-Weslake heads, valvetrain, camshaft, dry-sump or wet-sump system as fitted, Weber or alternate induction, ignition drive, exhaust, and engine installation.

## Current Verified State

Source S015 verifies that the Ford GT 40 No. 224 homologation package included a 1968 Weslake aluminium cylinder-head variant and a 4942.16 cc / 302 cu in engine evolution. Source S024 adds Gurney-Weslake official head-development and race-head identification context. The exact installed engine build is not verified locally.

For base GT40 engine identity context, S015 also records no cylinder sleeves fitted, 8 inlet ports, 8 exhaust ports, one inlet valve and one exhaust valve per cylinder, and main/big-end bearing journal diameters on the 289 base engine pages. These are homologation identity facts only; they are not rebuild clearances and they do not identify the installed 302.

## Working Assumptions

- Treat the engine as a high-output period racing small-block until proven otherwise.
- Assume Weber IDA-style induction only as a research placeholder.
- Assume no generic Ford 302 street-car torque, ignition, oiling, or carburetor settings apply without compatibility evidence.

## Critical Identification Checklist

- Block casting number, date code, main-cap arrangement, bore/stroke, and builder stamps.
- Cylinder head markings, port shape, valve sizes, chamber volume, and repair history.
- Cam card, rocker ratio, pushrod length, lash target, spring pressures, and installed height.
- Carburetor model, choke size, emulsion tubes, jets, air correctors, idle jets, pump jets, and float settings.
- Throttle linkage, throttle stops, pedal stop, butterfly-spindle return springs, and proof that throttles return freely from all positions.
- Ignition type, distributor curve, plug heat range, coil/box, and tach drive.
- Oil system layout, pump type, tank, cooler, filters, thermostat, line sizes, and pressure relief setup.

## 1968 FIA Appendix J Implications

Source S009 verifies that 1968 Group 4 engine recognition controlled identity-level details including cylinder head, cylinder block, cylinder count, bore, stroke, and crankshaft bearings. For this knowledge base, "Gurney-Weslake 302" must therefore be resolved into measured and documented hardware before any shop setting is accepted.

Source S009 also verifies that ignition, induction, exhaust, carburettor, filters, and manifolds were freer than the hard engine identity points, and that re-boring up to 1.2 mm was allowed in the regulation. This does not authorize a rebuild or tune-up value for this engine. Wall thickness, piston selection, compression, cam, head condition, fuel, and builder records still control the safe mechanical decision.

## Gurney-Weslake Head Identification Evidence

Source S024, the Gurney-Weslake official homepage, adds practical identification prompts for the cylinder-head question:

- The head project is described as originating from Dan Gurney's Ford Fairlane 289 cid alloy-head concept taken to Harry Weslake.
- The design cues described include 9-degree inclined inlet ports, round inlet ports, and Weslake-pattern heart-shaped chambers.
- The page distinguishes Mark I test heads, Mark II English heads, Mark III assembly/maintenance development, and Mark IV lighter heads with narrower rocker covers and inclined carburetor mounting.
- It draws a critical boundary between 1968 road-car cylinder-head castings and GT40 race heads: road-car heads were not fitted to GT40s at the time, while GT40 heads used William Mills high-grade full-race castings with full-race chambers, porting, and passageways.
- The road-car casting branch is a specific inspection hazard: S024 describes modified-design passenger heads with detuned chambers, budget design, LM8 material, Aeroplane and Motor Foundry castings, possible different port or valve sizes, and differing internal passageways.
- Later-converted road heads can be visually misleading. S024 says many were converted as near to race-car specification as the Gurney factory could make them for Indy-car use, and that Aeroplane and Motor cast heads were usually branded Gurney Eagle although some may have been retrofitted with Gurney-Weslake rocker covers.
- The S024 page includes race-car-head versus road-car-head comparison photo captions. Use those images only to guide the evidence photos a mechanic should capture; visual comparison does not replace casting/foundry marks, chamber/port/passageway evidence, measurements, or provenance.

Use S024 to improve the inspection checklist for casting/foundry marks, material/foundry branch, chamber form, port form, passageway clues, rocker-cover details, manifold angle, and documentary provenance. Do not use it for valve lash, plug reach, plug heat range, chamber volume, valve size, machining limit, torque value, compression ratio, or rev limit.

## Weber IDA Support Evidence

Source S018 adds an official Weber support path through Webcon, which identifies itself as an official Weber distributor and supplier of genuine Weber carburetors and spare parts. Webcon also provides Weber diagrams, tuning/service manual, and fitting-instruction categories, plus a Weber IDA category with 46/48 IDA service, gasket, and master rebuild kit support.

The inspected Webcon pages add concrete parts-source leads for verified Weber IDA installations:

- Literature categories for calibration sheets, exploded diagrams, fitting instructions, service manuals, tuning manuals, and Weber setting instructions.
- 46/48 IDA minor gasket set product code 9200340500.
- 46/48 IDA twin-barrel carburettor service kit product code 9216320500.
- 46/48 IDA twin-barrel master rebuild kit product code 9210500500.
- 48 IDA top-cover gasket product code 4170502100 and 48 IDA / 48 IDF base-gasket and heat-insulating-spacer listings.

Use S018 for:

- Identifying Weber IDA service-part families after the installed carburetor model is documented.
- Locating exploded diagrams, service kits, rebuild kits, jets, and Weber literature.
- Separating genuine Weber support data from generic web tuning tables.

Do not use S018 to infer that this car has Weber IDA carburetors, to select kit part numbers without inspecting the installed carburetor bodies, or to set jetting, fuel pressure, float level, pump volume, idle mixture, or linkage synchronization. Those settings remain blocked until the installed carburetor model/series, choke sizes, jet stack, linkage, fuel system, cam, compression, exhaust, and build documentation are known.

Source S019 adds a current historic safety gate for throttle return: each throttle butterfly spindle must have an external return spring, except double carburetors may alternatively use an internal spring. For the project car, record the installed carburetor family, spindle arrangement, linkage geometry, throttle stops, pedal stop, return-spring arrangement, and free-return behavior before carburetor adjustment, first start, or event-readiness claims.
