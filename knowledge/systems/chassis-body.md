# Chassis and Body

## Scope

Steel semi-monocoque/tub, front and rear structures, fiberglass body panels, doors, roof sections, latches, glazing, cooling apertures, underbody, and safety structure.

## Current Verified State

Source S015 verifies the Ford GT 40 No. 224 homologation research-copy baseline for wheelbase, front/rear track, recognized weight, and chassis/body material categories. No chassis number, tub inspection, body provenance, or original drawing set has been captured for the user's car.

## Known Risks

- GT40 Mk I cars, JWA lightweights, continuation cars, replicas, and later rebuilds can look similar while using different structure, materials, and repair practices.
- Rust, old race repairs, poorly documented panel replacement, and roll-cage changes can alter load paths.
- Door/roof fit and glazing alignment can hide tub twist or prior impact repair.

## Inspection Priorities

- Record chassis plate, stamping, and any body/tub tags before cleaning.
- Photograph suspension pickup points, bulkheads, sill structures, pedal box, fuel-cell areas, door apertures, and rear clip mounts.
- Measure diagonals and suspension pickup geometry before removing bonded/riveted structure.
- Separate preservation decisions from race-use safety updates.

## Linked Part Records

Use [../data/parts-register.csv](../data/parts-register.csv) before ordering parts or planning repairs:

- P001 - chassis plate and tub identifiers.
- P002 - steel tub panels and sponsons.
- P003 - fiberglass front and rear clips.
- P004 - doors, windows, seals, and latches.

Apply [../procedures/component-identification.md](../procedures/component-identification.md) before treating a tub, clip, door, or glazing detail as period-correct. Preserve all evidence of prior repair until chassis provenance is resolved.

## 1968 FIA Appendix J Implications

Source S009 verifies that Group 4 sportscar status was tied to recognition rules, a two-seat sportscar definition, and minimum-weight controls.

Source S015 provides the Ford GT 40 No. 224 research-copy baseline: 95 in / 2413 mm wheelbase, 55 in / 1397 mm front and rear track, 905 kg / 1993 lb recognized weight, partly unitized steel chassis/body construction, steel unitary material, and fiberglass/steel coachwork. Use these as recognition data, not as proof that the current car is straight, original, unrepaired, or built to that exact specification.
