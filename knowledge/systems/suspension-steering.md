# Suspension and Steering

## Scope

Control arms, uprights, hubs, bearings, springs, dampers, anti-roll bars, steering rack, column, joints, ride height, alignment, and corner weights.

## Current Verified State

Source S015 verifies base GT40 No. 224 recognition context for independent front and rear suspension with coil springs. It leaves front/rear stabiliser, shock-absorber count, shock-absorber type, steering type, servo-assistance, and lock-to-lock steering fields blank in the inspected copy. No installed suspension geometry, damper data, spring rates, steering rack data, or alignment setup has been captured.

## S015 Item-Number Boundaries

Use [../templates/suspension-steering-capture.md](../templates/suspension-steering-capture.md) when capturing shop evidence against these recognition-form prompts:

- Front suspension: item 70 type is independent; item 71 spring type is coil spring; items 72-74 for stabiliser, shock-absorber count, and shock-absorber type are blank in the inspected copy.
- Rear suspension: item 78 type is independent; item 79 spring type is coil spring; items 80-82 for stabiliser, shock-absorber count, and shock-absorber type are blank in the inspected copy.
- Steering: items 60-63 for steering type, servo-assistance, steering-wheel turns lock-to-lock, and servo-assistance details are blank in the inspected copy.
- Photographs D and E provide front/rear axle and suspension visual recognition anchors only. They are not geometry, spring, damper, anti-roll-bar, steering-ratio, or alignment specifications.

## Inspection Priorities

- Photograph all pickup points, spacers, shims, rod ends, steering arms, and anti-roll-bar links before adjustment.
- Record ride height and corner weights as found with fuel load and driver ballast noted.
- Identify dampers, springs, bars, wheel offsets, and tire construction before applying alignment targets.
- Check cracks and elongation around pickup points before setting alignment.

## Linked Part Records

Use [../data/parts-register.csv](../data/parts-register.csv) before moving shims, replacing rod ends, or applying alignment settings:

- P018 - front suspension.
- P019 - rear suspension.

Apply [../procedures/component-identification.md](../procedures/component-identification.md) before treating as-found geometry as original or safe. Record shim stacks and fastener orientation before any suspension teardown.

## 1968 FIA Appendix J Implications

Source S009 verifies that suspension principle and component function were recognition-sensitive for Group 4. Preserve shim stacks, upright markings, steering-arm details, pickup-point dimensions, spring/damper identification, and as-found alignment before changing geometry.

Source S015 confirms the recognized suspension principle as independent front and rear with coil springs. It does not provide alignment targets, ride height, corner weights, spring rates, damper specifications, anti-roll-bar settings, steering type, steering ratio, servo-assistance, or lock-to-lock turns.

## Do Not Guess

No camber, caster, toe, ride height, spring rate, damper, anti-roll-bar, steering ratio, steering stop, or bump-steer recommendation is actionable until current use and component configuration are known.
