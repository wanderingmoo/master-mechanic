# Evidence-Controlled Inspection and Commissioning

## Purpose

Use this before first start, post-restoration start, or after acquiring an unknown GT40 or GT40-style car.

## Workflow

1. Preserve evidence before cleaning or disassembly.
2. Photograph all identifiers, tags, castings, routing, shims, linkages, and safety-wire patterns.
3. Build the identity sheet in [../00-vehicle-identity.md](../00-vehicle-identity.md).
4. Work through the relevant rows in [../data/parts-register.csv](../data/parts-register.csv).
5. Apply [component-identification.md](component-identification.md) before ordering parts, applying settings, or machining.
6. Update the configuration state in [../data/configuration-register.csv](../data/configuration-register.csv).
7. Add each evidence-backed fact to [../data/fact-register.csv](../data/fact-register.csv).
8. Treat all fluids, rubber, fuel cells, brake seals, and safety equipment as suspect until inspected.
9. Perform dry mechanical checks before ignition or fuel enablement.
10. Enable fuel and ignition only after leak checks, oil pressure verification, throttle return verification, and fire safety are ready.
11. Record every change as a reversible setup note with source, date, and reason.

## First-Start Gates

- Engine can be barred through by hand without abnormal resistance.
- Oil pressure can be established without combustion if system design allows.
- Throttles return positively from every pedal and linkage position.
- Fuel pressure is verified with a known-good gauge.
- No fuel, oil, coolant, or brake leaks under static pressure.
- Fire extinguisher or fire system is armed and accessible.
- Cooling fans/pumps, if electric, are verified before heat soak.
