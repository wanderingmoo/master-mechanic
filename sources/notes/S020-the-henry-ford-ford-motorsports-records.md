## Extracted facts

- The collection consists of correspondence, press releases and other textual materials, photographs, proof sheets, and negatives.
- The records were maintained by the Ford Motor Company Lincoln-Mercury Public Affairs Department.
- The collection documents Ford Motor Company's involvement in racing and performance testing.
- The Events and Automobiles series covers 1957-1968 Ford-Lincoln-Mercury involvement in races, durability runs, and rallies.
- The collection is open for research.
- The collection is credited as a gift of Ford Motor Company.
- Keywords include Ford Motor Company, automobile racing, motorsports, sports cars, Dan Gurney, and 24 Hours of Le Mans.

## Mechanic-facing implications

- This is a credible archive path for period Ford racing, publicity, race-event, performance-testing, and Le Mans context.
- It may help locate period press releases, photographs, proof sheets, and textual records that clarify GT40 race configuration or Ford-supported claims.
- It does not by itself provide rebuild clearances, torque values, carburetor jetting, ignition timing, brake setup, tire pressure, suspension setup, or event readiness.
- Any item obtained from this collection must be separately cataloged with its own source note and extracted facts before being used as mechanic-facing evidence.

## Recommended acquisition query

Ask The Henry Ford for Ford Motorsports Records materials from 1968 related to Ford GT40, 24 Hours of Le Mans, John Wyer/JWA/Gulf, Gurney/Weslake cylinder heads, 302/4.9 litre engine, Ford performance testing, and any press releases or proof sheets that identify visible engine, induction, brake, wheel, or chassis details.

## What this source does not prove

- It does not prove the installed car's chassis identity or configuration.
- It does not prove that any specific Ford GT40 used a particular component unless the requested archival item directly states or shows it.
- It is an acquisition path, not an actionable service manual.
