## Extracted facts

- The FIA Historic Database identifies the car as Ford GT 40, Recognition/Homologation No. 224, Group 4.
- The FIA car page lists Ford as make, GT 40 as model, Group 4, 4736 cc, RWD, 8 cylinders, United Kingdom, and date of homologation February 2, 1966.
- The base form identifies Ford Advanced Vehicles Ltd. as manufacturer and records 50 identical cars reached by December 31, 1965.
- The base form records 95 in / 2413 mm wheelbase, 55 in / 1397 mm front track, 55 in / 1397 mm rear track, and 905 kg / 1993 lb weight.
- The base form records a partly unitized steel chassis/body structure, steel unitary material, fiberglass and steel coachwork, fiberglass bonnet, and fiberglass boot lid.
- The base form includes item 7 for fuel-tank capacity, including reserve, but the inspected copy does not populate that value.
- The base form includes wheel fields for type, weight per wheel, method of attachment, rim diameter, and rim width, but the inspected copy does not populate those wheel values.
- The base form records front suspension type as independent with coil springs and rear suspension type as independent with coil springs.
- The base suspension page includes stabiliser, number of shock absorbers, and shock absorber type fields for the front and rear suspension, but the inspected copy does not populate those values.
- The base chassis/coachwork page includes steering fields for steering type, servo-assistance, steering-wheel turns lock-to-lock, and servo-assistance details, but the inspected copy does not populate those values.
- The front-suspension section records item 70 as independent suspension and item 71 as coil spring; items 72 through 74 for stabiliser, number of shock absorbers, and shock absorber type are blank in the inspected copy.
- The rear-suspension section records item 78 as independent suspension and item 79 as coil spring; items 80 through 82 for stabiliser, number of shock absorbers, and shock absorber type are blank in the inspected copy.
- The chassis/coachwork page includes steering items 60 through 63 for steering type, servo-assistance, steering-wheel turns lock-to-lock, and servo-assistance details; those steering fields are blank in the inspected copy.
- The base form records hydraulic brake operation, but the inspected copy does not populate master-cylinder count, wheel-cylinder/caliper bore, disc diameter, disc thickness, lining/pad dimensions, number of pads, or swept/lining area values.
- The base form's wheel section contains items 50 through 54 for wheel type, per-wheel weight without tyre, method of attachment, rim diameter, and rim width, but the inspected copy leaves those fields blank.
- The base form's brake section identifies photographs F and G as brake photographs and item 90 as hydraulic brake operation. Items 91 through 105 cover servo-assistance, hydraulic master-cylinder count, cylinder count per wheel, wheel-cylinder/caliper bores, drum-brake dimensions, disc-brake outside diameter, disc thickness, brake-lining/pad dimensions, pad count, and area; the inspected copy leaves those values blank.
- The photograph sheet includes front and rear axle/suspension views plus close-up photographs of hub/brake assemblies. These are visual recognition anchors only, not dimensional brake or wheel specifications.
- The base engine entry records V8, 4-stroke, 101.6 mm bore, 72.9 mm stroke, 4736 cc / 289 cu in total capacity, cast-iron block, cast-iron heads, 5 main bearings, shell main and rod bearings, one centrally located camshaft in block, chain cam drive, and pushrod/rocker valve operation.
- The base engine page records no cylinder sleeves fitted, 8 inlet ports, 8 exhaust ports, 57.12 mm / 2.249 in crankshaft main bearing diameter, and 53.92 mm / 2.123 in connecting-rod big-end bearing diameter.
- The base four-stroke-engine page records one inlet valve and one exhaust valve per cylinder, but the inspected copy does not populate inlet/exhaust valve diameter, valve lift, spring, tappet-clearance, valve-timing, air-filter, carburetor, or injection values.
- The base engine page includes items 151 through 157 for lubrication system, lubricant capacity, oil cooler, engine cooling method, cooling-system capacity, cooling-fan diameter, and cooling-fan blade count, but the inspected copy does not populate actionable values for these systems.
- The engine-accessories page includes item 230 for fuel pump type, mechanical and/or electrical, and item 231 for number fitted, but the inspected copy does not populate those values.
- The engine-accessories page includes items 232 through 235 for ignition-system type, number of distributors, number of ignition coils, and number of spark plugs per cylinder, but the inspected copy does not populate those values.
- The engine-accessories page includes items 236 through 241 for generator type/count, generator drive method, generator voltage, battery count, battery location, and battery voltage, but the inspected copy does not populate those values.
- The base driveline entry records ZF manual gearbox, 5 forward ratios, offset bevel final drive, and limited-slip differential.
- Page 12 states that, for cars entered in Group 4 Sportscars, only a defined set of recognition-form items and photographs are to be considered during scrutineering. Treat this as a source-scope control, not as a full service checklist.
- The FIA car page lists extensions for cylinder head, 4942 cc engine, gearbox, and limited slip.
- The first variant amendment identifies a Weslake aluminium cylinder head for Ford GT 40, part number 3/3710, valid from January 1, 1968. The amendment page includes photographs of the engine unit from the right and left with clutch and accessories but without air filter or gearbox, plus a combustion-chamber/valve photograph.
- The engine evolution amendment records 101.6 mm bore, 76.2 mm stroke, 617.77 cm³ / 37.75 cu in capacity per cylinder, 4942.16 cm³ / 302 cu in total capacity, and crankshaft part number C8FE-6300-A, valid from May 1, 1968. The amendment page includes a crankshaft photograph with flywheel and damper attached.
- The gearbox / limited-slip evolution amendment records a Hewland LG600 5-speed gearbox and Ford heavy-duty limited-slip differential part number CDH4204A, valid from May 1, 1968. The amendment page includes a gearbox photograph.

## Mechanic-facing implications

- This source promotes the GT40 homologation-form target from open to captured research-copy status.
- A 1968 Gurney-Weslake 302 configuration now has primary FIA support as a homologated GT40 Group 4 variant/evolution, not merely a secondary-source lead.
- The homologation form provides identity and period-recognition data, not a full service manual. It does not provide safe torque, lash, ignition, jetting, oil, brake, tire, or alignment settings.
- The brake and wheel sections are useful as an evidence checklist, but their blank values mean the knowledge base must not infer calipers, discs, master cylinders, wheel dimensions, wheel attachment, center-lock torque, or tire pressure from S015.
- Photographs F and G may help a mechanic decide which installed hub/brake areas to photograph and compare, but they are not enough to identify caliper model, disc size, hub/peg-drive design, thread direction, center-lock torque, or service limits.
- The suspension and steering sections are useful as evidence checklists, but their blank values mean the knowledge base must not infer stabilizer bars, damper counts/types, steering type, steering ratio/turns, servo-assistance, ride height, or alignment values from S015.
- Item numbers 70-74, 78-82, and 60-63 should be used as shop-capture prompts for the installed front suspension, rear suspension, and steering systems.
- The fuel, oil, cooling, and electrical/accessory sections are also useful as evidence checklists, but their blank values mean the knowledge base must not infer fuel capacity, fuel-pump type, oil-system type, oil quantity, oil-cooler configuration, cooling-system layout, fan specification, ignition type, generator type/output, battery location, or battery voltage from S015.
- Item numbers 7, 151-157, and 230-231 should be used as shop-capture prompts for the installed fuel, oil, and cooling systems.
- Item numbers 232-241 should be used as shop-capture prompts for the installed ignition, charging, and battery systems.
- The 1968 amendments are directly relevant to the project configuration: Weslake aluminium heads, 302 cu in / 4942 cc engine, and gearbox/limited-slip evolution.
- The amendment photographs are useful visual anchors for identification conversations, but they do not replace physical marks, measurements, provenance records, or component manuals.
- Installed hardware must still be matched to the form by physical inspection and records before settings are issued.
