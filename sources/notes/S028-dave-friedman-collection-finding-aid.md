## Why this matters

S021 identifies the Dave Friedman Collection as an official The Henry Ford archive path for racing photographs, documents, programs, entrant lists, car-detail files, press kits, Ford Motor Company material, Shelby-American material, and Dan Gurney / All-American Racing material. S028 is the detailed finding aid that points to specific subgroups, series, boxes, and topics useful for GT40, Ford GT, Shelby, Le Mans, Daytona, Sebring, and Ford Motor Company racing research.

This is still an acquisition source. It identifies where relevant materials may exist; it does not itself provide GT40 repair settings or installed-car proof.

## Extracted facts

- The finding aid identifies the repository as Benson Ford Research Center, The Henry Ford, accession 2009.158.
- The finding aid describes the collection as 64.6 cubic feet, 93 negative binder boxes, 5 oversize boxes, and 100,000 digital images.
- Administrative information says the collection is open for research and gives a preferred citation format for items, folders, boxes, accession 2009.158, Dave Friedman collection, Benson Ford Research Center, The Henry Ford.
- Alternate forms include digitized material available through The Henry Ford Flickr collection and The Henry Ford collections site.
- The scope note says the collection includes race files with entrant lists, car details, programs, press kits, and published materials, plus material related to Ford Motor Company, Shelby-American International, and Dan Gurney's All-American Racing.
- The Negatives and Contact Sheets Shelby-American International subseries covers 1958-1968 and includes design, construction, and races of specialty prototype Ford GT cars including Mark II and Mark IV.
- The Prints / Ford Motor Company Racing subseries covers 1960-1970 and includes Ford endurance races for 1968-1969, GT and Mustang material, Mk. II / J-Car material, and Mk. IV material.
- The Research Files / Shelby Racing subseries covers 1962-1968 and includes race results, shipping records, FIA homologation reports, technical reports, and correspondence related to Shelby racecars as well as Ford GT and Indianapolis racecars.
- The container list identifies Ford Motor Company Racing, Box 116, Endurance 1968 folders including 1000 Km Monza, Nürburgring, Spa, 12 Hours of Sebring, 24 Hours of Daytona, 24 Hours of Le Mans, BOAC 500, and Watkins Glen 6-Hour.
- The container list identifies Ford Motor Company Racing, Box 117, GT Program folders including GT 40, 1964 FAV Slough Factory, 1965-1966 General, J-Car, 1966 Daytona Track Tests, 1966 Riverside Track Tests, and 1967 Dearborn Testing.
- The container list identifies Research Files / Shelby Racing, Box 160, as containing correspondence and reports from 1962-1968.
- The container list includes a Ford GT 40 Promotional Packet in the 2002 Programs and Printed Material sequence.

## Mechanic-facing implications

- Use S028 to target remote-research requests, not to answer service-setting questions.
- For period GT40 configuration work, prioritize the Ford Motor Company Racing endurance and GT Program folders, then the Shelby Racing research files.
- If The Henry Ford supplies item scans from these folders, each scan or packet must be assigned its own source ID before any claims are promoted into the fact register.
- Race photographs can support visual comparison and identification prompts, but installed-car identity still requires chassis, engine, transaxle, brake, wheel, and safety-system evidence from the project car.
