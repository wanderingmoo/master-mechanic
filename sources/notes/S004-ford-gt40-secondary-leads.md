## Extracted leads

- GT40P/1075 is identified as the Mk I chassis associated with the 1968 and 1969 Le Mans wins.
- The 1968 Le Mans-winning entry is described as a John Wyer Automotive Engineering car driven by Pedro Rodriguez and Lucien Bianchi with a Ford 4.9 L V8.
- The 1968-1969 GT40 context is tied to Group 4 sportscar eligibility and a 5.0 L ceiling after prototypes were limited to 3.0 L.
- The article points toward higher-value references: Ford press material, Ronnie Spain's chassis/race-record work, Gordon Bruce's Haynes GT40 manual, and ACO/Le Mans results.
