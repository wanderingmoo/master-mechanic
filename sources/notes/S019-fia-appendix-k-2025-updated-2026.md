## Extracted facts

- The FIA page lists `APPENDIX K 01.01.2025 updated 01.01.2026`.
- The PDF states application from January 1, 2025 and updated on January 1, 2026.
- Appendix K is the FIA technical regulation for cars competing in International Historic Competitions.
- Appendix K requires historic competition cars to respect period specification while also applying certain additional technical and safety provisions.
- Appendix K states that competitors must account for corrosion and ageing of components and preserve component integrity and safety while respecting original specification.
- Article 5 safety prescriptions are compulsory except on Regularity Rallies, where they are recommended.
- Presentation for scrutineering is treated as a declaration that the car is safe to compete.
- Battery-terminal short-circuit protection is mandatory; lithium-based batteries are prohibited.
- If a battery is in the cockpit, it must be dry type, securely fixed, and have an insulated leakproof cover.
- A general circuit breaker must cut electrical circuits other than those operating the fire extinguisher and must stop the engine; it must be spark-proof and accessible from inside and outside the car.
- Fuel, oil lines, and brake cables must be protected externally against deterioration risks and internally against fire risk.
- For Period F to K cars, unless period formula/championship rules require a higher standard, the fuel tank minimum requirement is safety foam meeting the specified anti-explosion standard or D-Stop foil; period or homologation maximum capacity must not be exceeded.
- FIA-approved safety tanks must comply with current Appendix J Article 253-14 when used.
- Quick-release Monza-type fuel caps protruding outside the body silhouette must have a secondary device to prevent accidental opening.
- Non-metallic pressurized fuel lines must be fire- and fuel-resistant; non-injected cars require a 100 psi minimum burst-pressure threshold.
- From Period F onward, fireproof bulkheads must be installed.
- Cars in all competitions must have at least one hand-operated fire extinguisher meeting current Appendix J Article 253-7.3.
- Oil catch tanks are required outside rallies: 2 litres minimum up to 2 litres engine capacity, 3 litres minimum above 2 litres, unless retained original closed-circuit breathing applies.
- Each throttle butterfly spindle must have an external return spring, except double carburetors may alternatively use an internal spring.
- For racing, two rear-view mirrors are required with at least 90 cm2 total glass area.
- For closed-circuit races, the glass of all front lamps must be taped or covered.
- Cars originally equipped with rear brake lights must have them working at the start of the race.
- A red rear warning light is required at race start and must be rear-facing, visible, securely attached, driver-switchable, and either an incandescent 21 W minimum or equivalent LED output.
- The red rear warning light must be less than 10 cm from the car centreline with a 20-40 cm2 area, or full-width bodywork may use two lights at equal distance from the centreline.
- Two-seat racing cars from Period F onward must use FIA 8853/98 or FIA 8853-2016 harnesses.
- Safety belts must not be anchored to seats or seat supports.
- Shoulder straps must run rearward/downward; Appendix K recommends a 10 degree angle and prohibits more than 45 degrees to horizontal from the upper backrest edge.
- FHR systems are compulsory in certain categories/periods and are strongly advised where physically possible for others.
- Lap and crotch straps must pass through the seat rather than over its sides, must hold the pelvic region, must not be worn over the abdomen, and must be protected from chafing against sharp edges.
- If shoulder or crotch straps cannot use series anchorage points, new anchorages must be installed on the shell or chassis; shoulder strap anchors should be as near as possible to the rear-wheel centreline.
- Appendix K defines reinforcement-bar and anchorage details for harness installation, including transverse tube size/material, M12 8.8 or 7/16 UNF bolt options, anchorage load requirements, and minimum steel reinforcement plate dimensions.
- Safety harnesses must be used in homologated configuration and according to manufacturer instructions.
- Belts must be replaced after severe collision, when webbing is cut/frayed/chemically or sun weakened, when metal parts/buckles are bent/deformed/rusted, or when the harness does not function perfectly.
- Appendix K Article 5.21 gives seat-replacement rules for listed GTS, CT, GT, and T categories from Period F onward; applicability to a GT40-style car must be checked against HTP/category/event classification before using those rules.
- Appendix K black-dot procedure can make a car absolutely non-conforming for safety and prevent it from competing until verified correction.

## Mechanic-facing implications

- This source gives the knowledge base a current FIA safety/event-readiness baseline beyond the 1968 Appendix J period benchmark.
- It should be used for inspection and event-preparation gates, not for generic street restoration claims.
- Installed safety equipment must be identified by make, model, date, certification, mounting, and routing before the assistant approves race/event readiness.
- For a 1968 GT40-style car, safety changes may be required even when they diverge from period appearance; document any originality tradeoff before modification.
- Always check the actual event supplementary regulations and the current FIA Appendix K page before competition use.

## What this source does not prove

- It does not prove the project car has a valid HTP, current event eligibility, or compliant safety equipment.
- It does not identify installed belts, extinguisher, fire system, fuel cell, battery, master switch, ROPS, mirrors, warning light, or fuel-line materials.
- It does not replace installed-component inspection or sanctioning-body/event-specific rules.
