## Why this matters

This source collects several Ford 302 and GT40-specific claims, but it is not primary engine documentation. Treat every engine claim here as a search lead until matched to Ford, FAV, JWA, Gurney-Weslake, Weslake, or engine-builder records.

## Extracted leads

- The 302 was derived by increasing 289 stroke, aligning with the general 302 cu in / about 4.9 L displacement context.
- The GT40 302 is described as using heavy-duty four-bolt main bearing caps, pressed-in core plugs, and Gurney-Weslake aluminum heads.
- The source distinguishes GT40 302 material from generic production-car 302 variants and later Tunnel-Port 302 material.

## Use constraints

- Do not use this source alone for block selection, machining limits, torque sequences, bearing clearances, compression ratio, camshaft specification, ignition timing, jetting, oiling, or rev limit.
- Use it to drive inspection of the actual block casting/date, main caps, core plugs, bore/stroke, heads, and build sheet.
